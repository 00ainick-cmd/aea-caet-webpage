AEA Registered Apprenticeship Program

A National Program Built for Avionics Shops

The AEA Registered Apprenticeship Program is a DOL-registered apprenticeship for Avionics Technicians (O*NET 49-2091.00). It gives repair stations a proven framework to train entry-level technicians into fully qualified avionics professionals — with structured on-the-job training, built-in Related Technical Instruction (RTI) through CAET certification, and compliance handled by AEA and Fastport.

Any AEA member shop can join the national program with a single Employer Acceptance Agreement — no lengthy registration process, no standalone program to build from scratch.

How the Apprenticeship Program Works

From enrollment to journey-level certification — here's how a shop moves through the program.

1
Join AEA

AEA membership is the gateway. Member shops gain access to the national apprenticeship program.

2
Sign the Agreement

One-page Employer Acceptance Agreement enrolls your shop in the DOL-registered national program.

3
Hire & Register

Bring on an apprentice. Fastport handles DOL registration and compliance documentation.

4
Train On the Job

Apprentice works in your shop under a mentor. CAET training satisfies the RTI requirement.

5
Earn Credentials

Apprentice earns CAET and CAET-Advanced certifications as milestones. Journey-level status upon completion.

CAET + Apprenticeship

CAET Certification is the RTI for the Apprenticeship

The AEA apprenticeship program uses CAET certification as its Related Technical Instruction (RTI) — the formal knowledge component required by the DOL alongside on-the-job hours. Apprentices earn their CAET, then advance to CAET-Advanced as a milestone in their journey to journey-level status.
